Overview

The GGBS framework for resilient water and urban systems

GGBS stands for Green–Grey–Blue–Smart infrastructure. It provides a simple, integrated way to think about how nature-based measures, conventional engineering works, water bodies, and data/analytics can be planned and operated together.

Urban drainage · flood resilience · stormwater management · rivers and coasts · decision support

About

What is the GGBS framework?

The GGBS framework groups infrastructure into four complementary dimensions:

  • Green – green infrastructure and nature-based solutions
  • Grey – conventional engineered systems and utilities
  • Blue – rivers, lakes, wetlands, coasts, and storage
  • Smart – sensing, modelling, control, and analytics

Instead of optimizing each dimension in isolation, GGBS emphasizes their combined performance under climate, land-use, and socio-economic change.

This site will gradually add concept notes, case studies, lecture materials, and tools as they become ready to share.

Framework

The four dimensions: Green, Grey, Blue, Smart

Green & Grey

Green measures include green roofs, rain gardens, bioswales, and permeable pavements that store, slow, and treat runoff while providing co-benefits such as vegetation, cooling, and amenity.

Grey systems include sewers, culverts, tunnels, levees, reservoirs, and coastal defenses that provide capacity and robustness. The framework highlights co-design of green and grey systems so they complement each other.

Blue & Smart

Blue infrastructure refers to rivers, lakes, wetlands, estuaries, coasts, and storage basins that convey and hold water, transport sediment, and support ecosystems.

Smart components include monitoring networks, data platforms, models, and (increasingly) AI-based analytics that support planning and real-time operations.

Applications

Selected application areas

  • Urban stormwater and “sponge cities” – coupling green infrastructure and drainage networks to reduce flooding and improve water quality.
  • Watershed and river-basin planning – coordinating reservoirs, levees, land use, and nature-based solutions at larger scales.
  • Coastal and estuarine systems – combining hard structures with soft/green and hybrid measures under sea-level rise.
