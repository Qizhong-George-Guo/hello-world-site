About the Framework

GGBS (Green–Grey–Blue–Smart) is an integrated framework designed to address the complexities of modern water management. It bridges the gap between traditional civil engineering (“grey”), nature-based solutions (“green” and “blue”), and data-enabled (“smart”) monitoring systems.

This platform serves as a hub for concepts, case studies, and research highlights focused on resilient sustainable water and infrastructure systems.

Our Vision

To advance the resilience of coastal and urban environments through:

  • Scientific Rigor: Deepening the understanding of hydrodynamics in complex environments.
  • Practical Solutions: Engineering flood risk reduction strategies that work in the real world.
  • Integrative Planning: Aligning hard infrastructure with ecological needs and community values.
  • Intelligent Systems: Utilizing AI and real-time data analytics for smarter decision-making.

Key Themes

  • Green stormwater infrastructure & sponge city concepts.
  • Urban and coastal flood resilience.
  • Integrated watershed and coastal management.
  • Coupling grey structures (breakwaters, levees) with nature-based solutions (reefs, wetlands).
  • Data, modelling, and intelligent control for water systems.
