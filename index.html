About GGBS

GGBS (Green–Grey–Blue–Smart) Infrastructure is an integrated framework that combines traditional civil engineering (“grey”) with nature-based solutions (“green” and “blue”) and data/AI-enabled “smart” systems.

This site will gradually evolve into a platform to share concepts, case studies, teaching materials, and research highlights on resilient and sustainable water and infrastructure systems.

Vision

The GGBS vision is to advance:

  • Scientifically rigorous understanding of urban and coastal water systems.
  • Practical engineering solutions for flood risk reduction and water quality improvement.
  • Integrative planning that aligns engineering, ecology, and communities.
  • Smart monitoring and decision-support tools, including data analytics and AI.

Key Themes

  • Green stormwater infrastructure and sponge city concepts.
  • Urban and coastal flood resilience.
  • Integrated watershed and coastal management.
  • Coupling grey structures with nature-based solutions.
  • Data, modelling, and intelligent control for water systems.

Future updates may include lecture notes, illustrative case studies, and interactive simulations.
