GGBS – Green–Grey–Blue–Smart Infrastructure Framework

The GGBS Framework provides an integrated way to plan, design, and manage water and urban infrastructure by combining green, grey, blue, and smart systems for resilient, sustainable, and adaptive cities, watersheds, and coasts.

Urban drainage & stormwater Watersheds & river basins Estuaries & coasts Flood resilience AI & intelligent systems

What Is the GGBS Framework?

Cities, rivers, and coasts face growing pressures from rapid development, aging infrastructure, extreme weather, sea-level rise, and evolving socio-economic conditions. Traditional approaches often treat green, grey, blue, and smart components as separate domains. The GGBS Framework provides an integrated, system-scale perspective.

GGBS emphasizes:

  • Synergy across green, grey, blue, and smart infrastructure;
  • Balancing nature-based and engineered solutions;
  • Recognizing water as both hazard and resource;
  • Using data, models, and intelligent systems for adaptive management;
  • Designing with climate variability and long-term change in mind.

The framework supports planning and operations across urban stormwater, watersheds, estuaries, and coastal environments, helping communities build resilience while improving environmental and social outcomes.

Four Complementary Dimensions

Green

Nature-based systems such as green roofs, rain gardens, bioswales, and permeable pavements that infiltrate, store, and treat runoff while providing co-benefits like cooling, habitat, and urban amenity.

Grey

Engineered systems including sewers, culverts, tunnels, levees, reservoirs, and coastal structures that provide capacity, robustness, and controlled conveyance for water.

Blue

Rivers, lakes, wetlands, estuaries, and coastal waters that transport water and sediment, store floodwaters, and support ecosystems and navigation.

Smart

Monitoring networks, hydrologic and hydrodynamic models, data platforms, optimization tools, and AI-enabled analytics for planning, forecasting, and real-time operation. This dimension incorporates the intelligent systems concepts previously developed under GGBI.

Applications

The GGBS Framework supports integrated solutions across a wide range of water and infrastructure challenges, including:

  • Urban stormwater and “Sponge City” practices;
  • Watershed and river-basin resilience planning;
  • Riverine, estuarine, and coastal processes and management;
  • Pluvial, fluvial, and coastal flood mitigation;
  • Nature-based and hybrid coastal protection measures;
  • Sediment transport and water quality management;
  • AI-enabled monitoring, modeling, and decision-support systems.

Future updates will add concept notes, case studies, instructional materials, and simplified modeling tools based on real-world applications.

About Professor Qizhong (George) Guo

Professor Qizhong (George) Guo is a civil and environmental engineer whose work spans hydrology, hydraulics, stormwater management, coastal processes, sediment transport, and resilient infrastructure. Over his academic career, he has led and contributed to studies on urban flooding, green infrastructure, watershed and river-basin planning, coastal protection, and nature-based solutions.

His research integrates green, grey, blue, and smart systems—an approach now synthesized into the GGBS Framework. Through scholarship, teaching, professional leadership, and mentoring, Professor Guo promotes rigorous science, practical engineering, and system-scale resilience thinking.

This website reflects his ongoing effort to organize, synthesize, and share concepts, case studies, models, and educational materials related to the GGBS Framework.
